Leo, also known and marketed as Leo: Bloody Sweet, is an upcoming Indian Tamil-language action thriller film[2] directed by Lokesh Kanagaraj, who co-wrote it with Rathna Kumar and Deeraj Vaidy. It is produced by S. S. Lalit Kumar of Seven Screen Studio while Jagadish Palanisamy serves as co-producer. The film stars Vijay and Trisha, alongside Sanjay Dutt, Arjun Sarja, Gautham Vasudev Menon, Mansoor Ali Khan and Mysskin.

The film was officially announced in January 2023 under the tentative title Thalapathy 67, as it is Vijay's 67th film as a lead actor, and the title of the film was announced a few days later. Principal photography commenced the same month in Chennai along with a sporadic schedule in Kashmir, which was again followed by another schedule held at the former location, and wrapped by mid-July. The film has music composed by Anirudh Ravichander, cinematography handled by Manoj Paramahamsa and editing by Philomin Raj. Leo is scheduled to be released in theatres worldwide on 19 October 2023 in standard, IMAX and other premium formats.


After the success of Master in early 2021, it was reported that Lokesh Kanagaraj visited Vijay at his residence and narrated a script which impressed him. The project was supposed to be the actor's 66th film as a leading actor; however, that position instead went to Varisu (2023).[15] Reports of a potential collaboration between Lokesh and Vijay continued through February 2022.[16][17] The following March, Lokesh confirmed there were discussions for him to direct Vijay's 67th film.[18] That May, he confirmed the project, tentatively titled Thalapathy 67.[19] The project was funded by S. S. Lalit Kumar's Seven Screen Studio, which had distributed and co-produced Master,[20] while Vijay's manager Jagadish serves as a co-producer. The company made a public announcement on 30 January 2023, confirming the project,[21] and the film's official title Leo was revealed on 3 February 2023.[22] The film is being made on a budget of ₹250–300 crore, and Vijay was reported to receive ₹120 crore (US$15 million) as remuneration.[1] However, he reportedly decided to take the revenue sharing model, to benefit the film's production; as per the basis, he would receive ₹50 crore (US$6.3 million) as an advance, and a chunk of the profits would be received as the actor's remuneration.[23] Vijay was not in favour of making Leo a pan-Indian film as he wanted to focus on satisfying the Tamil audience. The actor, however, changed his mind after Lalit and Jagadish convinced him. Some changes were also made to the original story to help the film cater to a wider audience.[24][25] Pre-production It took Lokesh nearly six months for the writing and pre-production.[26] Lokesh co-wrote the film's script along with Rathna Kumar, in his third consecutive collaboration with the director,[27][28] and Deeraj Vaidy, director of Jil Jung Juk (2016). Both of them joined the writing team in mid August 2022.[29] Lokesh said the film would be completely made in his style, unlike Master which he considered a 50% Vijay film and 50% his film.[26] A muhurat puja was held on 5 December 2022 at AVM Studios in Chennai with the film's cast and crew.[30] On 7 December, the team shot the promotional video for the film for nearly three days.[31] In mid-January 2023, Manoj Paramahamsa was announced as cinematographer, marking his first collaboration with Lokesh and third with Vijay after Nanban (2012) and Beast (2022).[32] Anirudh Ravichander would score the music, in his third consecutive film with the director.[33] Lokesh further retained most of his norm technicians including editor Philomin Raj, stunt choreography duo Anbariv, dance choreographer Dinesh Kumar and art director N. Sathees Kumar.[34] Praveen Raja would style the costumes for Vijay, along with his regular costume designers Pallavi Singh and Eka Lakhani.[35][36] Ramkumar Balasubramanian was appointed as executive producer of the film by the production house.[37] Casting

Vijay would sport a grey-shaded hairstyle for his character, which was finalised after 30 different looks.[38][39] Trisha was cast as the female lead,[40] pairing opposite Vijay for the fifth time after Ghilli (2004), Thirupaachi (2005), Aathi (2006) and Kuruvi (2008).[41][42] Bollywood actor Sanjay Dutt was confirmed to play one of the main antagonists, in his Tamil film debut as well as his second South Indian film after KGF: Chapter 2 (2022).[43] Dutt agreed to be a part of the project, after being impressed by the one-line narrated for the film.[44] Priya Anand also joined the film in an important role,[45] whilst Arjun was also cast in an important role.[46] He called it a "different" and "fresh" one that would justify his title "action king".[47] Mysskin,[48] Sandy Master,[49] Gautham Vasudev Menon,[50] and Mansoor Ali Khan would play prominent roles.[51] Malayalam actor Mathew Thomas, would also make his Tamil debut with the film.[52] All of their inclusion were confirmed by the production house in late-January 2023.[53] Manobala and Janany Kunaseelan were present at the film's preliminary shooting, confirming their presence in the film;[54][55] the latter would make her acting debut in a feature with this film.[54] George Maryan was also featured in the muhurat puja, thereby confirming his inclusion.[56] Abhirami Venkatachalam and Babu Antony were also confirmed after participating in the film's Kashmir schedule.[57][58] Manobala died on 3 May 2023, making Leo a posthumous release for him.[59]
